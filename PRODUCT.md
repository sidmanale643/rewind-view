# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers who use multiple AI coding assistants and need to recover context from past work. They use Rewind during active development to locate a previous session, read its transcript, or understand usage across providers.

## Product Purpose

Rewind indexes local AI assistant sessions from Claude, Codex, OpenCode, and Antigravity into one searchable interface. It succeeds when developers can quickly find the right session, understand what happened, and resume work without manually searching provider-specific files.

## Brand Personality

Fast, precise, understated. The interface should feel dependable and familiar to developers, with dense information that remains easy to scan.

## Anti-references

Avoid ornamental SaaS dashboards, oversized vanity metrics, decorative gradients and glows, card-heavy layouts, animated page entrances, and novelty interactions that slow down retrieval or reading.

## Design Principles

- Optimize for retrieval: search, filtering, and session identification take priority.
- Make transcripts comfortable to read during sustained technical work.
- Keep usage analysis useful and secondary to the core session workflow.
- Prefer familiar developer-tool interactions over decorative novelty.
- Preserve density without sacrificing hierarchy, accessibility, or keyboard flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard navigation, visible focus states, reduced motion, readable contrast, and provider or status distinctions that do not depend on color alone.
